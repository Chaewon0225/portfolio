NURTURE

Collaborators: Fengfeng Ke, Nuodi Zhang, Alex Barrett

We aim to support individuals with neurodiversity in rural areas through this project. We have completed interviewing 26 teachers, caregivers, and healthcare providers on this issue, and currently working on fully analyzing the interview data we collected.

While we're analyzing the interview, we're also investigating how we can design human-Ai collaboration in qualitative coding, both inductive and deductive. I present two conference proceedings based on this work.

From Code to Insight: How LLMs Help and Hinder Qualitative Research (LINK)

I compared four large language models and human performance in inductive qualitative coding. The results of graph neural network showed that certain models struggled to capture the holistic context of the interview, demonstrating limitations in comprehension and contextual analysis compared to human coders.

LLM-supported Thematic Analysis: Evaluating GATOS Workflow on Complex Qualitative Data (PDF)

GATOS workflow is one of the frameworks for human-AI collaboration on automating qualitative coding process. We used our authentic data to validate the process and suggested a few refinements to reflect the complexity of authentic qualitative dataset.
