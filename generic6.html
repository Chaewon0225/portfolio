Data Mining

I have been applying various educational data mining methods to gain insights about nursing students' learning experiences in the educational escape rooms. Unlike the typical pre-and post-test or ad-hoc survey often used in nursing education literature, such approach provides evidence to inform the optimal design practice for nursing educators who want to design an immersive learning experience without much technological expertise.

Sequential Pattern Mining

I used sequential pattern mining to analyze students' collaborative problem-solving dynamics in the data I collected in 2023. This was when I was applying epistemic network analysis (ENA) to map students' collaborative conceptualizations and realized that ENA does not capture the temporal dynamics.

The paper published in Clinical Simulations in Nursing leveraged this technique to discover meaningful three-step patterns of facilitators' stop-and-go debriefing in the educational escape rooms designed for first-year nursing students.
